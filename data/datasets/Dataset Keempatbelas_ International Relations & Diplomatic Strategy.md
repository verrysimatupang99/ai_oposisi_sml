2. **INDONESIA'S FOREIGN POLICY ARCHITECTURE**

**Historical Evolution dan Doctrinal Foundations**

Sukarno Era (1945-1965)

- **Independent and Active Policy**: Non-alignment principle, anti-colonialism, South-South cooperation, Bandung Conference leadership
- **Confrontation Politics**: Malaysia confrontation, West Irian campaign, anti-Western rhetoric, revolutionary diplomacy
- **Non-Aligned Movement**: Third World leadership, Cold War neutrality, anti-imperialism, solidarity movements
- **Asian-African Conference**: Bandung spirit, decolonization support, racial equality, peaceful coexistence principles
- **Revolutionary Diplomacy**: Ideological foreign policy, anti-status quo, radical rhetoric, confrontational approach

Suharto Era (1966-1998)

- **ASEAN Founding**: Regional stability, economic development, anti-communist containment, conflict prevention
- **Western Alignment**: US partnership, World Bank cooperation, foreign investment attraction, market economy
- **Quiet Diplomacy**: Low-profile approach, behind-scenes negotiation, consensus building, face-saving solutions
- **Development Diplomacy**: Economic priorities, investment promotion, technology transfer, capacity building
- **Regional Leadership**: ASEAN centrality, conflict mediation, consensus facilitation, institutional building

Reform Era (1998-present)

- **Democratic Foreign Policy**: Parliamentary oversight, civil society input, transparency increase, accountability mechanisms
- **Human Rights Integration**: International standards, domestic reforms, regional cooperation, norm internalization
- **Global Engagement**: G20 membership, climate leadership, peacekeeping contributions, multilateral activism
- **Middle Power Diplomacy**: Bridge-building, mediation, norm entrepreneurship, coalition leadership, global governance
- **Maritime Axis**: Ocean diplomacy, connectivity promotion, maritime security, blue economy, archipelagic state identity

ASEAN Centrality
