- **Leadership**: Din Syamsuddin, Haedar Nashir, Yunahar Ilyas, progressive Islamic thought

**Political Islam: Partai-Based Organizations**

- **Ideological Foundation**: Ikhwanul Muslimin influence, Islamic state aspiration, gradual Islamization strategy
- **Cadre System**: Tarbiyah movement, systematic recruitment, ideological training, disciplined organization

- **Declining Influence**: Electoral marginalization, leadership crisis, organizational weakness, identity confusion
- **Factional Struggles**: Romahurmuziy vs Suharso factions, corruption scandals, internal conflicts
- **Survival Strategy**: Coalition partnerships, patronage politics, regional strongholds, Islamic symbolism

- **Violence Capacity**: Paramilitary training, intimidation tactics, mass mobilization, security threats
- **Dissolution Impact**: Member dispersion, splinter groups, underground activities, radicalization risks

Jamaah Islamiyah (JI)

- **Terrorist Network**: Bali bombings, regional jihad, Al-Qaeda connections, violence legitimization
- **Organizational Structure**: Cell-based operations, international funding, training camps, recruitment networks
- **Ideological Framework**: Takfiri doctrine, jihad obligation, Islamic state establishment, anti-Western sentiment
- **Counter-Terrorism Response**: Security operations, deradicalization programs, international cooperation
- **Current Status**: Weakened but persistent, splinter groups, lone wolf attacks, online radicalization
